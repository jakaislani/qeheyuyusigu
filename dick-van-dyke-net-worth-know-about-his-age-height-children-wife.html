Van was fascinated by his mother’s religious family in his youth and even thought of making his career in the government but a single drama class in high school changed his mentality of getting involved in the ministry.

In his autobiography, he mentioned, “I suppose that I never completely gave up my childhood idea of being a minister.

Only the medium and the message changed. I have still endeavored to touch people’s souls, to raise their spirits and put smiles on their faces.”

The actor left high school in 1944. However, he continued school and received a diploma at the age of 84.

Although he was determined to join the pilot training in the United States Army Air Forces during the World War II, he had to abandon it as he was denied for being underweight.

Dick Van Dyke – Movies and TV shows

Dick van Dyke is a well-known figure in the entertainment industry. Dyke started in NBC first as a comedian and later made appearances on “James Chance of a Lifetime” in 1954.

Later, he appeared in two episodes of “The Phil Silvers Show”, “The Pat Boone Chevy Showroom” on ABC and “The Polly Bergen Show” on NBC.

Dyke, even being involved in the showbiz, had taught Sunday school in the Presbyterian Church in his early years, where he explained the importance of religion in everyday life.

He has published “Faith, Hope, and Hilarity: A Child’s Eye View of Religion”, a book which is solely based on his experiences as a Sunday School teacher.

Van Dyke had also been principal in “KXIV Inc.” and owned 1400 AM KXIV in Phoenix from 1965 to 1985.
