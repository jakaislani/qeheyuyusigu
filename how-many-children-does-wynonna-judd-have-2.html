Wynonna Ellen Judd was born Christina Claire Ciminella on May 30, 1964, in Ashland, Kentucky, the United States to parents Naomi Judd and Charles Jordan.

Wynonna Judd’s biological father, Charles Jordan died in 2000. She has a younger half-sister Ashley Judd who is an actress. She first gained the attention of the media in the 1980s alongside her mother Naomi in the country music duo the Judds.

Wynonna Judd and her mother Naomi had released seven albums on Curb Records before disbanding in 1991. She has earned her place among the most widely recognized and awarded female country singers.

Wynonna Judd was inducted into the Country Music Hall of Fame as a member of The Judds in 2021. She has had 19 No. 1 singles making her one of the best-selling country artists of all time.
