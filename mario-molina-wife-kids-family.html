Through this article, let’s get to know more about Mexican chemist Mario Molina wife, kids, and family. Mario Molina is a name that played an essential role in the discovery of the Antarctic ozone hole.

Mario Molina was a Mexican chemist born on March 19, 1943, in Mexico City, Mexico, and passed away on October 7, 2020.

He was known for his pioneering work in atmospheric chemistry and his role in discovering the threat posed by chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) to the Earth’s ozone layer.

In the mid-1970s, the chemist began researching the impact of CFCs on the ozone layer. He and his research partner, Sherwood Rowland, discovered that CFCs could break down ozone molecules in the Earth’s stratosphere, leading to a hole in the ozone layer.

Their research contributed to the signing of the Montreal Protocol in 1987.

For his work on atmospheric chemistry and the ozone layer, Mario was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1995, becoming the first Mexican-born person to receive the award.
